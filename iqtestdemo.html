IQ Test Demonstration

An interactive classroom activity showing how intelligence-test items can measure different cognitive skills, how scores are calculated, and why test results require careful interpretation.

Important: This is not a real IQ test and does not produce a valid IQ score. It is a teaching demonstration designed to help students understand item types, scoring, time pressure, and interpretation issues in psychological testing.

What this demonstrates

This activity includes sample items that resemble common cognitive-testing categories: verbal reasoning, numerical reasoning, spatial reasoning, pattern recognition, working memory, and processing speed.

After the demo, students receive a score summary and reflection prompts about reliability, validity, culture, anxiety, and the limits of testing.

How to use it

Students answer 12 short questions. A timer is included to demonstrate how time limits can influence performance, but the score is based only on answers.

Teacher Notes

Suggested discussion questions:

  • Which questions seemed to measure learned knowledge versus reasoning?
  • How might language, schooling, test anxiety, disability, or culture affect scores?
  • Why is one short online quiz not enough to estimate intelligence?
  • What would make a psychological test reliable and valid?
